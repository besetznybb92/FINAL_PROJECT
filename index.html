Established in 1986, J.O.B. Music Connection specializes in providing individual an group music therapy services to those with developmental and intillectual disabilities specializing in unique and rare medical disorders.

Jeanne O. Besetzny

Jeanne O. Besetzny, Music Therapist-Board Certified, holds a Master of Arts in Music Therapy from St. Mary-of-the-Woods College, IN (2002); advanced training in: Neurologic Music Therapy (2017); DIRFloortime for Music Therapists (2020); Music Together (2018); Raising Harmonies, Sprouting Melodies: Music Therapy, and Early Childhood: A Developmental Approach (2020); Individual Music-Centered Assessment Profile for Neurodevelopmental Disorders, (IMCAP-ND)(2019); a Bachelor of Music in Music Therapy from DePaul University (1985)(Chicago, Il); Certificate in Mental Health Education from the Institute for Psychoanalysis, Chicago, Il (1989); as well as Orff Schulwerk Training: Level I (1983), Level II (1992), Level III (1995) DePaul University, Chicago Illinois. A full-time Music Therapy Clinician since January 1986, Jeanne has worked at The Institute for Therapy through the Arts, a program of the Music Institute of Chicago, Winnetka, Il.; Little City Foundation, a residential placement program for developmentally delayed children ages 3-21 years old in Palatine Illinois; the Children’s Habilitation Center, Harvey, Il. While maintaining a full-time status, Jeanne developed a Music Therapy Private Practice. As Founding Director of J.O.B. Music Connection, Inc., Jeanne consults to facilities who enhance their service programs with the addition of Music Therapy Services, including 1:1 Sessions, Small and Large Group Sessions and Community Based Life Skills programs such as Musical Theatre and Swing Choirs. Agencies such as: Community Alternatives Unlimited (Chicago, Il.); St. Mary of Providence Residential Program (Chicago, Il.); The Suzuki-Orff School for Young Musicians (Chicago, Il.); the Illinois Spina Bifida Association Summer Camp (Lake Forest, Il); The Rehabilitation Institute of Chicago (Chicago, Il.); Evanston Hospital Physical Medicine and Rehabilitation Unit (Evanston, Il.); Tragedy Assistance Program for Survivors (T.A.P.S.) United States Military Grief Survivors Memorial Weekend Camp for Children (Washington D.C.); Oak Grove School (Special Education District of Lake County, Il.) and the Ravinia Festival Out Reach Program (Deerfield, Il) have dedicated time and funds to support long-term contractual services. Short-term consultation completed on an “as-needed” basis to facilities such as: Ebinger School (Chicago Public School); Edison Park Lutheran Church Music & Arts Camp (Chicago, Il.) and Evanston Special Recreation Program. Within her business, Jeanne enjoys educating others about the benefits of music therapy services, developing new music therapy programs and assisting music therapists in creating their own private practices. Jeanne is a Past-President(1994-1996) of the Illinois Association of Music Therapy (President 1990-1994). She is a member of the American Music Therapy Association (1986-present), serving as a Delgate (1994-1996). A past Vice-President of the Great Lakes Region (GLR) of the American Music Therapy Association (1992-1994) and past GLR Representative to the International Relations Committee of AMTA (1988-1992). Jeanne has completed numerous presentations at State, Regional and National Conferences on topics related to Private Practice and sample case studies. In her free time, she is a professional flautist and recorderist, performing and teaching within the Greater Chicago area.
